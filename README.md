## Redistribution Attitudes by Party and Education
This project explores how individuals' preference and attitudes towards redistribution policies 
varies by political party and level of education using data from the General Social Survey (GSS).
This analysis looks at spending preferences on social security, welfare, education, environmental
protection and health. The analysis was conducted using R. 

---

## Analysis Overview
The analysis is broken up into three main parts:
1. Breakdown of Education Groups by Party Affiliation
This section looks at the number of Democrats and Republicans in each education group using
lollipop plots 

2. Redistribution Attitudes by Party and Education
This section calculates and compares the mean attitudes towards the five categories of government 
spending across education level -- broken up by political party. This is done with faceted line
plots. 

3. Overall Attitude Change Over Time
This section compares overall attitudes on the five categories of government spending from 2010 and
2024 to assess if overall preferences have changed over time. 

--- 

## Required Packages
The analysis was done using R, and requires the following packages to reproduce:
- tidyverse
- ggplot2
- haven
